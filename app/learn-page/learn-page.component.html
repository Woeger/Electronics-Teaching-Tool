Components

Here you'll find some of the components in the beta version of the app, with a handy little description!

Wire

The most basic type of component in a circuit, and probably the most important one! Wires connect components with each other to allow current to pass through them. They have two ends and will pass current between each side.

Bulb

Without these, we'd still be using oil lamps! Invented in 1879, bulbs light up when enough current is passed through them.

Cell

The powerhouse of any circuit. Cells take many shapes an sizes but they all share one thing in common, they provide power to a circuit.

Resistor

Resistors are for when there is too much power! They create resistance which helps regulate a circuit.

Current

Current is how much electrical charge is flowing through a circuit. While a circuit might have so much power, not all of that might not flow through part of a circuit due to resistance.

Resistance

Resistance is what reduces the flow of current in a circuit. You can calculate this by dividing the potential difference by the current at a point in the circuit. All components will have a tiny amount of internal resistance, but we have special components called resistors that can produce a lot more.
